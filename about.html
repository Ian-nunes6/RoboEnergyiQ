About RoboEnergiQ

RoboEnergiQ is a solo research & development project by Ian Nunes that explores how robotics can be designed and controlled to use less energy without sacrificing performance.

The project studies energy usage at multiple levels ‚Äî sensor selection, actuation planning, motion smoothing, timing, and task sequencing. The goal is to quantify the power savings achievable by software and simple hardware optimizations.

As a concrete demonstration, a QR-based sorting arm has been implemented to show energy comparisons between baseline and optimized strategies. The sorting demo is a practical testbed, not the primary objective ‚Äî the central aim remains reducing energy use in robotic systems.
